# Common Lab Test Reference Notes

## Complete blood count
Hemoglobin, hematocrit, red blood cell count, white blood cell count, and platelets are common CBC values. Low hemoglobin can suggest anemia or blood loss, while high or low white blood cells can happen with infection, inflammation, medications, or bone marrow conditions. Platelet changes can affect bleeding or clotting risk.

## Blood sugar
Fasting glucose and HbA1c help estimate blood sugar control. High values can suggest diabetes or prediabetes, but interpretation depends on fasting status, repeat testing, pregnancy, medicines, and symptoms.

## Kidney function
Creatinine, blood urea nitrogen, and estimated glomerular filtration rate are used to assess kidney filtration. Dehydration, muscle mass, diet, medicines, and chronic kidney disease can affect these values.

## Liver enzymes
ALT, AST, alkaline phosphatase, bilirubin, albumin, and total protein are common liver-related results. Abnormal values can come from liver inflammation, bile duct problems, alcohol, medicines, viral infections, or non-liver causes.

## Lipid profile
Total cholesterol, LDL, HDL, and triglycerides estimate cardiovascular risk. LDL is often called bad cholesterol, HDL is often called good cholesterol, and triglycerides can rise with diet, diabetes, alcohol, and genetic factors.

## Thyroid tests
TSH, free T4, and sometimes free T3 are used to assess thyroid function. High TSH can fit with underactive thyroid, while low TSH can fit with overactive thyroid, but the full pattern matters.

## Urine tests
Urine reports may include protein, glucose, ketones, blood, nitrites, leukocyte esterase, and microscopic cells. Abnormal urine findings can reflect infection, kidney issues, stones, contamination, dehydration, or diabetes-related changes.

## Safety symptoms
Urgent medical attention is important for chest pain, severe shortness of breath, fainting, confusion, severe weakness, signs of stroke, very high fever, severe dehydration, heavy bleeding, or rapidly worsening symptoms.
