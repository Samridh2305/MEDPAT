Liver Function Test (LFT) Knowledge Base Guide
Overview

Liver Function Tests (LFTs) are a group of blood tests used to assess:

Liver cell health
Bile flow and drainage
Protein synthesis by the liver
Metabolic function
Liver inflammation or injury

LFTs help identify:

Hepatitis
Fatty liver disease
Alcohol-related liver disease
Cirrhosis
Bile duct obstruction
Drug-induced liver injury
Liver failure
IMPORTANT NOTE FOR AI INTERPRETATION

Despite the name "Liver Function Test", not all LFT parameters measure liver function directly.

LIVER ENZYMES
Alanine Aminotransferase (ALT / SGPT)
What it Measures

ALT is an enzyme found primarily inside liver cells.

Clinical Importance

Most liver-specific enzyme.

Elevated ALT May Indicate
Fatty liver disease
Viral hepatitis
Drug-induced liver injury
Alcohol-related liver disease
Autoimmune hepatitis
Mild Elevation

<5× upper limit

Moderate Elevation

5–15× upper limit

Severe Elevation

15× upper limit

AI Interpretation

High ALT generally indicates liver cell injury.

Aspartate Aminotransferase (AST / SGOT)
What it Measures

AST is found in:

Liver
Heart
Muscle
Kidneys
Clinical Importance

Less specific than ALT.

Elevated AST May Indicate
Liver disease
Muscle injury
Heart injury
Alcohol-related liver disease
Interpretation Rule

AST should always be interpreted alongside ALT.

AST/ALT Ratio
Formula

AST ÷ ALT

Clinical Patterns
AST/ALT < 1

Often seen in:

Fatty liver disease
Viral hepatitis
AST/ALT > 2

Strongly suggests:

Alcohol-related liver disease
AST/ALT > 1

May occur in:

Cirrhosis
Advanced fibrosis
AI Interpretation

The AST/ALT ratio provides important clues about the type of liver injury.

CHOLESTATIC MARKERS
Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP)
What it Measures

ALP is present in:

Bile ducts
Bone
Placenta
Intestine
Elevated ALP May Indicate
Liver Causes
Bile duct obstruction
Gallstones
Cholestatic liver disease
Non-Liver Causes
Bone disorders
Healing fractures
Growth in children
Interpretation Rule

Always interpret ALP with GGT.

Gamma-Glutamyl Transferase (GGT)
What it Measures

Enzyme associated with liver and bile ducts.

Elevated GGT May Indicate
Alcohol use
Fatty liver disease
Cholestasis
Drug-induced liver injury
Important Pattern
ALP ↑ + GGT ↑

Suggests liver or bile duct origin.

ALP ↑ + Normal GGT

Suggests bone-related cause.

BILIRUBIN
Total Bilirubin
What it Measures

Bilirubin is produced when red blood cells break down.

The liver processes and excretes bilirubin.

Elevated Bilirubin May Cause
Jaundice
Yellow eyes
Dark urine
High Bilirubin Causes
Liver disease
Hemolysis
Bile duct obstruction
Direct (Conjugated) Bilirubin
What it Measures

Processed bilirubin ready for excretion.

Elevated Direct Bilirubin

Common causes:

Bile duct obstruction
Hepatitis
Cholestatic disease
Indirect (Unconjugated) Bilirubin
What it Measures

Unprocessed bilirubin.

Elevated Indirect Bilirubin

Common causes:

Hemolysis
Gilbert syndrome
Excess RBC breakdown
PROTEIN SYNTHESIS MARKERS
Albumin
What it Measures

Main protein produced by the liver.

Functions
Maintains fluid balance
Transports substances in blood
Low Albumin May Indicate
Chronic liver disease
Cirrhosis
Malnutrition
Kidney disease
Severe inflammation
High Albumin

Usually due to dehydration.

Clinical Importance

One of the best indicators of long-term liver synthetic function.

Total Protein
What it Measures

Albumin + Globulins

Low Total Protein

May indicate:

Liver disease
Malnutrition
Kidney disease
High Total Protein

May indicate:

Chronic inflammation
Certain blood disorders
Globulin
What it Measures

Immune-related proteins.

Elevated Globulin

May occur in:

Chronic liver disease
Autoimmune hepatitis
Chronic infections
Albumin/Globulin Ratio (A/G Ratio)
Formula

Albumin ÷ Globulin

Low Ratio

May suggest:

Chronic liver disease
Autoimmune disease
Chronic inflammation
COAGULATION (IF AVAILABLE)
Prothrombin Time (PT)
What it Measures

Blood clotting efficiency.

Clinical Importance

Liver produces many clotting factors.

Prolonged PT

May indicate:

Severe liver dysfunction
Vitamin K deficiency
INR
What it Measures

Standardized clotting measurement.

Elevated INR

Suggests impaired liver synthetic function.

Clinical Importance

One of the strongest indicators of severe liver disease.
