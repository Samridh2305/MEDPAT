Complete Blood Count (CBC)
Overview

A Complete Blood Count (CBC) is one of the most commonly ordered blood tests. It evaluates the three major blood cell types:

Red Blood Cells (RBCs) – carry oxygen.
White Blood Cells (WBCs) – fight infections.
Platelets – help blood clot.

CBC results help detect:

Anemia
Infections
Inflammation
Blood cancers
Immune disorders
Bleeding disorders
Nutritional deficiencies
RED BLOOD CELL PARAMETERS
Hemoglobin (Hb)
What it Measures

Hemoglobin is the oxygen-carrying protein inside red blood cells.

Clinical Importance

Determines oxygen transport capacity.

High Hemoglobin May Indicate
Dehydration
Smoking
High altitude living
Polycythemia
Chronic lung disease
Low Hemoglobin May Indicate
Iron deficiency anemia
Vitamin B12 deficiency
Folate deficiency
Blood loss
Chronic kidney disease
Bone marrow disorders
Typical Adult Range

Male: 13.5–17.5 g/dL

Female: 12–15.5 g/dL

Severity Classification
Mild Anemia

10–12 g/dL

Moderate Anemia

8–10 g/dL

Severe Anemia

<8 g/dL

Red Blood Cell Count (RBC)
What it Measures

Number of red blood cells per volume of blood.

High RBC
Dehydration
Polycythemia
Chronic hypoxia
Low RBC
Anemia
Blood loss
Bone marrow suppression
Interpretation Notes

Should always be interpreted with:

Hemoglobin
Hematocrit
MCV
Hematocrit (HCT / PCV)
What it Measures

Percentage of blood volume occupied by red blood cells.

High Hematocrit
Dehydration
Polycythemia
Low Hematocrit
Anemia
Blood loss
Overhydration
Relationship

Generally rises and falls alongside hemoglobin.

RED CELL INDICES

These help classify anemia.

Mean Corpuscular Volume (MCV)
What it Measures

Average size of red blood cells.

Interpretation
Low MCV (<80 fL)

Microcytic anemia

Common causes:

Iron deficiency
Thalassemia
Chronic disease
Normal MCV (80–100 fL)

Normocytic anemia

Common causes:

Acute blood loss
Kidney disease
Chronic disease
High MCV (>100 fL)

Macrocytic anemia

Common causes:

Vitamin B12 deficiency
Folate deficiency
Alcohol abuse
Liver disease
Clinical Reasoning

MCV is often the first parameter used to classify anemia.

Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin (MCH)
What it Measures

Average amount of hemoglobin in each RBC.

Low MCH

Usually indicates:

Iron deficiency
Microcytic anemia
High MCH

Usually seen in:

Macrocytic anemia
Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin Concentration (MCHC)
What it Measures

Concentration of hemoglobin inside RBCs.

Low MCHC

Hypochromic anemia

Common causes:

Iron deficiency
High MCHC

Less common:

Hereditary spherocytosis
Severe burns
Hemolysis
Red Cell Distribution Width (RDW)
What it Measures

Variation in RBC size.

High RDW

Indicates mixed RBC sizes.

Common causes:

Iron deficiency
B12 deficiency
Folate deficiency
Mixed anemia
Normal RDW

Cells are relatively uniform in size.

Important Pattern
High RDW + Low MCV

Strongly suggests iron deficiency anemia.

Normal RDW + Low MCV

Suggests thalassemia trait.

WHITE BLOOD CELL PARAMETERS
White Blood Cell Count (WBC)
What it Measures

Total number of infection-fighting white blood cells.

High WBC (Leukocytosis)

Possible causes:

Bacterial infection
Inflammation
Stress
Steroid use
Leukemia
Low WBC (Leukopenia)

Possible causes:

Viral infection
Autoimmune disease
Chemotherapy
Bone marrow suppression
DIFFERENTIAL WBC COUNT
Neutrophils
Role

Primary defense against bacterial infections.

High Neutrophils

(Neutrophilia)

Common causes:

Bacterial infection
Acute inflammation
Steroid therapy
Stress response
Low Neutrophils

(Neutropenia)

Common causes:

Viral infections
Chemotherapy
Bone marrow disease
Clinical Pattern

High WBC + High Neutrophils

→ Strongly suggests bacterial infection.

Lymphocytes
Role

Important in viral immunity.

High Lymphocytes

(Lymphocytosis)

Common causes:

Viral infections
Mononucleosis
Chronic lymphocytic leukemia
Low Lymphocytes

(Lymphopenia)

Common causes:

HIV
Steroids
Severe illness
Clinical Pattern

High lymphocytes often suggest viral infection.

Monocytes
Role

Remove damaged tissue and pathogens.

High Monocytes

Common causes:

Tuberculosis
Chronic infections
Autoimmune diseases
Low Monocytes

Usually less clinically significant.

Eosinophils
Role

Involved in allergies and parasitic infections.

High Eosinophils

Common causes:

Asthma
Allergic disorders
Drug reactions
Parasitic infections
Clinical Pattern

High eosinophils often correlate with allergy-related symptoms.

Basophils
Role

Participate in allergic and inflammatory responses.

High Basophils

May occur in:

Chronic inflammation
Myeloproliferative disorders
Allergies
Low Basophils

Usually not clinically important.

PLATELET PARAMETERS
Platelet Count (PLT)
What it Measures

Number of platelets available for blood clotting.

High Platelets (Thrombocytosis)

Possible causes:

Iron deficiency
Infection
Inflammation
Certain cancers
Low Platelets (Thrombocytopenia)

Possible causes:

Dengue
Viral infections
Immune thrombocytopenia
Bone marrow disorders
Risk Categories
Mild

100–150 ×10⁹/L

Moderate

50–100 ×10⁹/L

Severe

<50 ×10⁹/L

High Bleeding Risk

<20 ×10⁹/L

Mean Platelet Volume (MPV)
What it Measures

Average platelet size.

High MPV

Indicates younger platelets.

Seen in:

Increased platelet destruction
Recovery after bleeding
Low MPV

May indicate reduced platelet production.
